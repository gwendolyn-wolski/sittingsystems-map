Sources

A working bibliography for chairs, posture, interiors, and seated life.

Section 01

Books

Foundational texts on chairs, seated form, typology, and the social life of furniture.

Cranz, Galen. The Chair: Rethinking Culture, Body, and Design. New York: W.W. Norton, 1998.

A critical analysis of seated form, posture, and the cultural assumptions embedded in chair design.

Gamper, Martino. 100 Chairs in 100 Days and Its 100 Ways. London: Dent-De-Leone, 2007.

A study in iterative chair-making through reuse and modification of existing forms.

Section 02

Media

Films, interviews, and visual references that shape the archive’s reading of chair history and seated space.

Bütler, Heinz, dir. Chair Times: A History of Seating from 1800 to Today. HOOK Film & Kultur Produktion GmbH in cooperation with the Vitra Design Museum, 2018. First published 2019.

A filmed timeline of seating design drawn from 125 objects in the Vitra Design Museum collection, tracing developments from the early nineteenth century to contemporary digitally fabricated forms.
